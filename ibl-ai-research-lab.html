Stage 1

Ask & Research

Formulate a rigorous research question, build search strategies, locate and extract evidence from academic sources, and organise knowledge into a structured map.

🔬 9 Steps ⏱ 1–2 Weeks 📚 Academic Sources
Step 0
0
Candidate Research Questions
Generate and explore multiple candidate research questions before selecting one
Goal: Before committing to one research question, generate 4–6 candidates across different epistemological angles. Evaluate each against feasibility, scope, and academic interest.
🛡️ ETHICAL TIP — Candidate Questions
AI-generated questions are starting points, not conclusions. Evaluate each candidate yourself before selecting — ask: Is this genuinely researchable? Is the scope achievable? Does it reflect my team's actual interest? The selection decision must remain fully human.
⚡AI Module — Question Generator
NEW STEP · Required
Step 1A
1A
Final Research Question Synthesis
Compare all candidate RQs and construct one rigorous, theoretically coherent final question
When to use: After generating candidate questions in Step 0. Before beginning epistemological analysis (Step 1B). This step ensures your final RQ is not merely chosen but actively constructed through systematic comparison of all candidates.
🛡️ ETHICAL TIP — Final RQ Synthesis
The AI helps you compare and synthesise — but it cannot understand your team's disciplinary context, available resources, or genuine scientific curiosity. Before running this step: each team member should independently rank the candidates and share their reasoning. The AI output is a structured argument, not a verdict. Final selection belongs to the team.
📋 Evaluation Framework: The AI will compare your candidates against 8 dimensions: (1) alignment with core phenomenon, (2) inclusion of key variables, (3) representation of mechanisms, (4) epistemological quality, (5) scientific rigour, (6) individual strengths/weaknesses, (7) synthesis of strongest elements, (8) final validation criteria.
⚡AI Module — Final RQ Constructor
Step 1B
1B
Epistemological Analysis
Deep-analyse your final research question from Step 1A
🛡️ ETHICAL TIP — Epistemological Analysis
Use the AI's analysis to test your question, not to replace your own thinking. If the AI suggests a refined version of your RQ, discuss it as a team before accepting it. Never adopt AI-suggested scope changes without understanding why they improve the question.
⚡AI Module — Epistemological Analyser
Step 2
2
Search String Builder
Generate Boolean search strings for academic databases
🛡️ ETHICAL TIP — Search Strings
Boolean strings generated by AI may be too complex for some databases or produce zero results. Always test each string manually before trusting it. Simplify any string that returns fewer than 5 or more than 500 results. The search strategy is yours to own — AI provides drafts, not finished tools.
⚡AI Module — Search Strategy
⛔ Automatic Exclusions: The following source types are not accepted in this framework and must be rejected regardless of relevance: Wikipedia · Studocu · SlideShare · Course Hero · personal blogs · grey literature without institutional authorship. Always verify journal credibility before extracting evidence.
🛡️ ETHICAL TIP — Source Evaluation
AI evaluates sources based on the information you provide. Always supply DOIs where available — the AI cannot independently verify journal impact factors or retraction status. If a source has no DOI and no ISSN, treat it with extreme caution. Cross-check at least 2 sources using Scimago or Sherpa Romeo.
⚡AI Module — Source Evaluator
Step 4
4
Evidence Extractor
Automatically extract and structure evidence from abstracts or papers
🛡️ ETHICAL TIP — Evidence Extraction
AI extracts from what you provide — it cannot access the full paper. If you only paste an abstract, the extraction will be limited to what the abstract states. Never assume the AI found something the text does not say. Always cross-check extracted findings against the original paper. If there is a discrepancy, the paper wins.
⚡AI Module — Evidence Extraction
Step 5
5
Source Selection & CRAAP Analysis
Apply the CRAAP test and select your 5 best sources
🛡️ ETHICAL TIP — Source Selection
A source that scores well on the CRAAP test is still your responsibility. CRAAP evaluates credibility — you must additionally assess relevance to your specific RQ. A highly credible source in the wrong topic area should not be included just because it is well-rated. Final source selection is a human judgment, not an algorithmic output.
⚡AI Module — CRAAP Tester
Step 6
6
Topic & Subtopic Mapper
Organise your evidence into a coherent thematic structure
🛡️ ETHICAL TIP — Topics & Subtopics
The topic map should reflect your evidence, not a generic overview of the field. Before accepting an AI-generated structure, ask: Does each subtopic correspond to at least one source I actually read? If a subtopic appears with no source anchor, either find a source or remove it. AI may default to textbook categories — push beyond them.
⚡AI Module — Topic Organiser
Step 7
7
Mind Map Generator
Generate a structured mind map outline and Mermaid diagram code
🛡️ ETHICAL TIP — Mind Maps
Mind maps generated by AI may include connections that sound plausible but are not supported by your evidence. Before finalising your map, check every node and connection: Can I point to a specific source that supports this relationship? Delete any node without a literature anchor. The structure of knowledge must reflect your sources, not AI's general patterns.
⚡AI Module — Mind Map
Step 8
8
Scientific Glossary Builder
Automatically build a rigorous glossary from your research terms
🛡️ ETHICAL TIP — Glossary
Include only terms that genuinely appear in your research sources. Remove any AI-added jargon that is not relevant to your topic. Each definition should be traced back to your sources — if you cannot link a term to at least one paper you read, question whether it belongs in your glossary. Quantity is not the goal; precision is.
💡 Simplified mode available: If technical definitions feel too advanced, use the Simple + Advanced option — each entry will have both a plain-language explanation and a formal academic definition.
⚡AI Module — Glossary Generator

Step 10

Multimodal Output Generator
Generate structured scaffolds for any output format — each with a distinct visual and communicative identity
🛡️ ETHICAL TIP — Multimodal Outputs
Visuals and scripts scaffolded by AI must be verified against your evidence before production. AI-generated images or diagrams may misrepresent biological or scientific structures — always compare against peer-reviewed figures or authoritative atlases. Your output communicates science: any inaccuracy becomes misinformation for your audience.
🎨 Format Differentiation Guide: Each format has a distinct identity — 10A Poster = spatial density + visual hierarchy · 10B Podcast = narrative arc + voice register · 10C Videocast = scripted pacing + visual sequencing · 10D Game = mechanic loop + learning objective · 10E Oral = live delivery + audience interaction. Choose the format that best matches your message, not your comfort zone.
